Vahid R. Asadi

My name is Vahid Reza Asadi, and I'm a third-year Ph.D. student at the Cheriton School of Computer Science and the Institute for Quantum Computing, University of Waterloo. I'm very fortunate to have Mohammad Hajiabadi and Richard Cleve as my advisors. Previously, I got my M.Sc. in Computer Science from Simon Fraser University under the wonderful supervision of Igor Shinkar. Before that, I got my B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from the University of Tehran.

In summer 2023, I was a visiting Ph.D. student at EPFL hosted by Alessandro Chiesa.

Research Interests

I'm generally interested in theoretical computer science. More specifically, my prime interest lies in complexity theory, quantum information, and foundations of (post-quantum) cryptography. I'm also interested in algorithmic coding theory and interactive proofs.
