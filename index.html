About Me

My name is Vahid Reza Asadi and I'm a first-year Ph.D. student at the Cheriton School of Computer Science and the Institute for Quantum Computing, University of Waterloo. I'm very fortunate to have Mohammad Hajiabadi and Richard Cleve as my advisors. Previously, I got my M.Sc. in Computer Science from Simon Fraser University, under the wonderful supervision of Igor Shinkar. Before that, I got my B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from the University of Tehran.

Research Interests

I'm generally interested in theoretical computer science. More specifically, my prime interest lies in (post-quantum) cryptography, quantum information, complexity theory, and the interplay between them. I'm also interested in algorithmic coding theory and differential privacy.
