Vahid R. Asadi

My name is Vahid Reza Asadi, and I am a final-year Ph.D. student at the Cheriton School of Computer Science and the Institute for Quantum Computing, University of Waterloo. I am fortunate to be advised by Richard Cleve and Mohammad Hajiabadi. Previously, I received my M.Sc. in Computer Science from Simon Fraser University, where I was supervised by Igor Shinkar. Before that, I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from the University of Tehran.

I was a visiting Ph.D. student at EPFL in the summer of 2023, hosted by Alessandro Chiesa, and a visiting researcher at Nagoya University in the summer of 2025, hosted by François Le Gall.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in theoretical computer science, with a primary focus on complexity theory and quantum computation, and their connections to cryptographic problems such as interactive proofs.
